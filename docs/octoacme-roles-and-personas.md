# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Clarify requirements, discuss technical trade-offs, and provide estimates
- **Project Managers**: Report progress, identify risks, and coordinate dependencies
- **QA/Testing**: Collaborate on test strategies, review test coverage, and verify bug fixes
- **UX Designers**: Implement designs, provide feedback on feasibility, and validate UI/UX
- **DevOps Engineers**: Work on CI/CD improvements, deployment automation, and infrastructure needs
- **Scrum Master**: Participate in agile ceremonies, report blockers, and contribute to retrospectives

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Align on delivery timelines, dependencies, and resource needs
- **Developers**: Discuss technical feasibility, clarify requirements, and prioritize work
- **QA/Testing**: Define acceptance criteria and success metrics for features
- **UX Designers**: Collaborate on user research, validate design solutions against user needs
- **Stakeholder Group Lead**: Coordinate stakeholder feedback and requirements
- **Stakeholders**: Present roadmap, gather input, and measure product success

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align on priorities, timelines, and scope trade-offs
- **Developers**: Track progress, remove blockers, and coordinate cross-team dependencies
- **QA/Testing**: Monitor testing progress, ensure quality gates are met
- **Scrum Master**: Coordinate on agile ceremonies and complement process facilitation with project coordination
- **DevOps Engineers**: Schedule releases, track deployment risks and dependencies
- **Stakeholder Group Lead**: Facilitate stakeholder communication and manage expectations
- **Stakeholders**: Provide status updates, escalate issues, and seek approvals

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA/Testing professionals ensure that software meets quality standards, acceptance criteria, and is free of defects. They validate functionality, usability, and performance before releases.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategies and test plans for features
- Write and execute manual and automated tests
- Validate acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Report, track, and verify defects
- Participate in planning and refinement to ensure testability
- Collaborate on test automation and CI/CD integration

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria and user expectations
- Reduce manual testing overhead through automation
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report testing status and blockers
- PR reviews to validate test coverage and quality
- Bug reports and test results documentation
- Test plan reviews with Product Managers and Developers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Review PRs for test coverage, collaborate on test automation, and verify bug fixes
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria, ensure features meet user requirements
- **Project Managers**: Report testing progress, risks, and blockers in status updates
- **DevOps Engineers**: Integrate automated tests into CI/CD pipelines
- **UX Designers**: Conduct usability testing and validate UI/UX implementation

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders are individuals or groups with an interest in the project's outcome. They provide requirements, feedback, and approvals, and may include executives, customers, or business units.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business requirements and priorities
- Review and approve key deliverables and milestones
- Offer feedback on features and user experience
- Make go/no-go decisions at critical gates
- Support resource allocation and budget decisions

### Goals
- Ensure the project delivers expected business value
- Protect customer and business interests
- Maintain visibility into project progress and risks
- Enable informed decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Monthly status updates and milestone reviews
- Stakeholder briefings and roadmap presentations
- Approval requests for major decisions or changes
- Feedback sessions and acceptance reviews

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align on priorities, provide market and customer insights
- **Project Managers**: Review status reports, approve scope changes, escalate issues
- **Stakeholder Group Lead**: Channel feedback and requirements through designated representatives
- **Scrum Master**: Participate in sprint reviews and demos

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They ensure the team follows agreed processes and continuously improves.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, retrospectives, and reviews
- Remove blockers and impediments to team progress
- Coach the team on agile principles and best practices
- Shield the team from external distractions
- Track and improve team velocity and process metrics
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Ensure adherence to agile principles and ceremonies
- Create a safe environment for team collaboration and feedback
- Drive continuous process improvements

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standups and team coordination
- Weekly coordination with Project Managers on progress and risks
- Retrospective summaries and action item tracking
- Coaching conversations with team members

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate on project timelines, dependencies, and escalations; complement PM's focus on delivery with agile process facilitation
- **Developers**: Remove impediments, facilitate collaboration, and support velocity tracking
- **Product Managers**: Ensure backlog readiness and clarify priorities during planning
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinate testing activities within sprints and ensure quality gates
- **DevOps Engineers**: Align on deployment schedules and CI/CD pipeline improvements

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape the user experience vision through research, design, and usability testing. They ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and delightful to use.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature requirements
- Ensure design consistency and accessibility standards
- Provide design hand-offs with clear specifications to Developers
- Validate implementation matches design intent

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and user-centered experiences
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Align design decisions with user needs and business goals

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques with cross-functional teams
- Usability testing results and user research findings
- Design hand-off documentation and specification reviews
- Collaboration sessions with Developers and Product Managers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align on user needs, prioritize design work, and validate solutions
- **Developers**: Provide design specifications, collaborate on implementation, and review UI/UX
- **QA/Testing**: Partner on usability testing and acceptance validation
- **Stakeholders**: Present design concepts and gather feedback
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate design milestones and dependencies

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure automation, and operational reliability. They enable rapid, safe deployments and maintain production systems.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code and automation
- Monitor production systems and respond to incidents
- Implement security scanning and compliance checks
- Optimize deployment processes and reduce cycle time
- Share best practices for observability and reliability

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and automated deployments
- Maintain high availability and system reliability
- Reduce manual operational overhead
- Ensure security and compliance in infrastructure

### Typical Communication
- Incident reports and postmortems
- Infrastructure and deployment documentation
- CI/CD pipeline improvements and release coordination
- On-call handoffs and runbook updates

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide CI/CD support, infrastructure tooling, and deployment guidance
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate release schedules, report deployment risks and dependencies
- **QA/Testing**: Integrate automated tests into pipelines, support test environments
- **Scrum Master**: Align on deployment cadence within sprint cycles
- **Product Managers**: Communicate deployment capabilities and constraints

---

## Stakeholder Group Lead

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Group Leads serve as primary points of contact for specific stakeholder groups, channeling requirements, feedback, and concerns to the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Represent and advocate for their stakeholder group's needs
- Consolidate and prioritize feedback from multiple stakeholders
- Communicate project status and updates to their group
- Facilitate stakeholder participation in reviews and approvals
- Escalate critical concerns or blockers to leadership

### Goals
- Ensure stakeholder needs are clearly communicated and understood
- Streamline stakeholder engagement and reduce noise
- Maintain alignment between stakeholder expectations and delivery
- Enable efficient decision-making and approvals

### Typical Communication
- Regular updates to stakeholder groups (newsletters, meetings)
- Feedback consolidation and prioritization sessions
- Escalation of critical issues to Project Managers and leadership
- Participation in milestone reviews and demos

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Provide consolidated stakeholder feedback, coordinate on communication plans
- **Product Managers**: Channel user and business requirements, validate priorities
- **Stakeholders**: Represent group interests, communicate project status
- **Scrum Master**: Participate in sprint reviews to provide stakeholder perspective
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria from stakeholder viewpoint
